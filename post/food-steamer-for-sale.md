<h3>Retaining Nutrients</h3>
<p>One of the primary benefits of steaming food is nutrient retention. When you cook vegetables using methods like boiling, many nutrients leach into the water. Steaming, on the other hand, allows food to cook in its own juices and moisture. This results in a meal rich in vitamins and minerals. For instance, steaming broccoli can keep significantly more vitamin C compared to boiling it.</p>
<h3>Low Fat Cooking</h3>
<p>Another advantage of using a food steamer is the ability to prepare low-fat meals. Unlike frying or sautéing, steaming does not require additional oils or fats. If you're looking to reduce calorie intake or maintain a healthy weight, a food steamer can be your best friend. You can enjoy flavorsome dishes without the guilt of added fats.</p>
